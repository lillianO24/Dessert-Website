Origins

Yakgwa (약과) is a delicious, cookie-like dessert that originated in Korea during the era of the three kindoms (around 57 B.C to A.D. 668). It was popular during the Goryeo era, where it was used for rituals, greetings, and marriage.

What is it?

Also known as gwajul (과줄), is a dessert that falls under the umbrella of yumil-gwa desserts. This means that it is a deep fried, wheat based confection. This sweet was originally offered during ancestral rites and eaten on special holidays such as chuseok, the harvest fesitval, or marriages.
