Origins

Tanghulu (糖葫芦) has been a popular dessert for around 800 years in China, and it appeared around the Song Dynasty. This particular period was known for innovations in the culinary area, resulting in this amazing snack. Tanghulu is also very significant in cultural heritage. It was often used during culinary practices and was also used for medicinal purposes as well. One of the more popular ingredients, hawthorn berries, are known for health benefits and are used in Chinese medicine. However, overtime, more fruits were popularized for tanghulu as well. Recently, it has become a very popular dessert that is often seen on different social media platforms such as tiktok. Not only is it delicious, it is also super easy to make, and you've probably seen numerous videos of people doing it at home!

What is it?

As previously mentioned, tanghulu is a type of candied fruit that is extremely easy to make. It started out with hawthorn berries, but people often use grapes, strawberries, and tangerines as well. Once the fruit is placed on a skewer, it is dipped in a boiling mixture of sugar and water. Once the fruit is coated, it is placed in ice water where it hardens immediately. The process is very simple, but if the sugar is at the wrong temperature when the fruit is dipped, the result might be very sticky! People often aim for a glass-like texture that results in the satisfying crunch when bitten into.
